#### STEM Applications (03/03/2026)

Application
- Academic (GPA), SAT, AP: 40%
	- Academic specialization: Honors, Awards, etc.
- Extracurricular: 30% 
- Essay, etc.: 30%

Every Ivy League admit has one thing in common: they've won multiple awards in high school competitions or received prestigious recognition for their achievements. For students aiming for top-tier universities, having a completed portfolio of honors and awards is not optional -- it is essential. 
- When most students applying have perfect grades and great SAT results, honors and competition wins help to show who's really on the top. 
- The absence of 5 great honors can indicate that student is weaker than the rest. 

Common App allows to open a demo app. 

Honors and Awards section of the Common App gives you the chance to showcase your most significant achievements outside of your school. It allows you to list up to 5 instance of external recognition.
- Honors should be ordered by prestige. You are not given space to describe the honor, meaning the more universally recognizable it is, the better. 
- Note: Honors won't win overnight -- it needs to develop over time.  
- Note: take competitions best suited for the talents. E.g.  International Science Fair (math modeling) instead AMC
- Note: honors / awards start from grade 9 in the app, but worth starting early 
- Try everything before high school (no need to prepare). Competition is an info game. 
	- Essay competition is open to everyone. 
	- The more you do OUTSIDE the class, the MORE you have over your classmates / competitors
	- Doing competition can make you better in that field

Importance of Research (Indigo Program)
- Define your research goals: participation in research illustrates academic competence and eagerness to learn outside the classroom.
- Steps (15-week): preliminary work / Reading & Analysis / Research Prospectus / Literature Review / Research & Write / Revise & Prepare / Submit Paper / Present at Conference

Note: Research is probably more valuable than competition
- Research papers can be counted as honors
- Preparation for a competition doesn't show if not winning, but research always can be told as a story or experience
- Certain schools explicitly ask for research experience
